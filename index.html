About Me

Successful project outcomes make me thrill – I appreciate the knowledge the most when the moment numbers and formulas intertwine and lead to a positive result. Being a utilitarian person, I love to utilize theories in subjects, such as mathematics, statistics, and computer science, and work out the problems. Even for most of the time, I was only asked to enhance product performance, and I would challenge the traditional ways of thinking and try to revolutionize our current business practices drastically.

Currently, I am a graduate student in the Master of Science in Business Analytics at USC Marshall School of Business. I consider myself an enthusiastic and analytical thinker with great ability to quickly understand or learn any statistic/machine learning models and apply them to solve real-world problems while utilizing powerful tools like Python and Excel.

I am interested in securing an internship in the data analytics field or any other area related to data. I wish to find a place where I will be able to leverage my knowledge gained from academic training to make realistic contributions while learning more advanced skills or techniques during the internship!
